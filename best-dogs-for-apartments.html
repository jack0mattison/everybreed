Best Dogs for Flats & Apartments (2026)

Living in a flat or apartment in the UK doesn't rule out dog ownership. The best flat dogs are typically smaller, relatively quiet and able to get enough exercise with daily walks and indoor play. Avoid breeds that need large gardens or are prone to excessive barking.

Consider your building rules, neighbours and access to outdoor space. Even small dogs need regular toilet breaks and mental stimulation. The breeds below are often well suited to apartment life when their needs are met.

1. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

Small, quiet and adaptable. Happy with moderate walks and indoor cuddles.

Size: Small · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: Moderate

2. French Bulldog

Low exercise needs and relatively quiet. Can overheat in summer—short walks and cool interiors.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: Low

3. Pug

Compact and companionable. Avoid overexertion and heat; suited to smaller spaces.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: Low

4. Cockapoo

Friendly and flexible. Can adapt to flat life with daily walks and play.

Size: Small–Medium · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: Moderate–High

5. Shih Tzu

Small lap dog that doesn't need a garden. Requires regular grooming.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: High

6. Miniature Dachshund

Small and manageable. Needs moderate exercise and care with backs (no jumping off furniture).

Size: Small · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: Low

7. Yorkshire Terrier

Tiny and portable. Can be vocal; training and routine help in flats.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: High

8. Whippet

Calm indoors despite being a sighthound. Needs a safe off-lead run; otherwise quiet at home.

Size: Medium · Exercise: Moderate–High · Grooming: Low

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you have a dog in a flat in the UK?

Yes. Many breeds adapt well to flat living provided they get daily walks, mental stimulation and appropriate toilet breaks. Check your lease and consider neighbours when it comes to barking.
