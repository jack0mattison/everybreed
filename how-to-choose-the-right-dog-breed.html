How to Choose the Right Dog Breed for Your Lifestyle

EveryBreed · 2026

Choosing the right dog breed is one of the most important decisions you'll make. A breed that fits your lifestyle, space and experience is more likely to be a happy, healthy companion for years. This guide gives you a simple framework and links to our breed and category pages so you can narrow down your options.

Assess your lifestyle

Start with honesty about your daily routine. How much time can you give to exercise, training and grooming? Do you have a garden, or are you in a flat? Do you work from home or are you out most of the day? Do you have children or other pets? Answers to these questions will rule in or out many breeds. For example, best dogs for flats tend to be smaller and quieter; best dogs for runners need high energy and stamina.

Key factors: size, energy and grooming

Size affects space, transport and cost (food, insurance). Small dogs like Cavalier King Charles Spaniels or Cockapoos suit many homes; large breeds like Labradors or German Shepherds need more room and exercise. Energy levels must match what you can provide—under-exercised dogs can develop behaviour problems. Grooming ranges from low (e.g. Whippet) to high (e.g. long-coated breeds); if you don't have time for daily brushing, choose a shorter coat or budget for professional grooming.

First-time owner or experienced?

Some breeds are more forgiving of training mistakes and easier to manage. See our best dogs for first-time owners for breeds that are typically biddable and adaptable. If you have experience, you might consider more demanding breeds such as Border Collies or German Shepherds—but only if you can meet their needs.

Use our breed and comparison pages

Browse our full breed list and open the guides for breeds you like. Each guide covers temperament, size, exercise, health, grooming and UK costs. If you're torn between two breeds, use our comparison pages (e.g. Labrador vs Golden Retriever) to see differences side by side.

Decision checklist

  • Can I provide the exercise this breed needs?
  • Do I have space (and budget) for this size of dog?
  • Can I commit to the grooming requirements?
  • Is this breed typically good with children/other pets if that applies?
  • Can I afford the puppy price and ongoing costs?

Once you've shortlisted breeds, visit responsible breeders, meet the dogs and ask about health testing and socialisation. The right breed is the one that fits your life and that you can care for for the rest of the dog's life.
