Labrador vs Golden Retriever: Which Breed Is Right for You?

Both are among the UK's most popular family gundogs. Here's how they compare on temperament, size, health, grooming and UK costs.

Temperament Compared

Labradors and Golden Retrievers share a friendly, people-pleasing temperament. Both are typically excellent with children and other pets when well socialised. Labradors can be slightly more boisterous and food-obsessed; Golden Retrievers are often described as slightly softer and more sensitive. Neither makes a good guard dog; both thrive as family and working companions.

Size & Appearance Compared

Both are large gundogs. Labradors are often stockier with a shorter, denser coat (black, yellow or liver). Golden Retrievers have a longer, flowing coat in shades of gold and cream. Size ranges overlap; Goldens can be slightly taller but both need space and regular exercise.

Exercise & Activity Compared

Both breeds need substantial daily exercise—at least an hour, often two, of walks and play. They enjoy fetch, swimming and off-lead running. Under-exercised, either can become bored and destructive. UK weather suits both; they cope well with rain and cold.

Health & Lifespan Compared

Both are prone to hip and elbow dysplasia; buy from health-tested breeders. Labradors can be prone to obesity and PRA; Goldens have higher rates of certain cancers and heart conditions. Average lifespans are similar (around 10–13 years). Pet insurance is recommended for both.

Cost of Ownership Compared

UK puppy prices are similar: £1,000–£2,500 for a well-bred Labrador, £1,200–£2,800 for a Golden. Ongoing costs (food, insurance, vet, grooming) are comparable—roughly £70–£150 per month for basics. Both are large dogs so food and insurance tend to be at the upper end.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose a Labrador if you want a slightly more robust, often more energetic gundog with a shorter coat (though still heavy shedder). Choose a Golden Retriever if you prefer a slightly gentler demeanour and don't mind more coat care. Both are excellent family dogs; the best choice often comes down to personal preference and availability from a responsible breeder.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which sheds more, Labrador or Golden Retriever?

Both shed heavily year-round. Golden Retrievers have longer hair so it may be more visible; Labradors have a dense undercoat. Regular brushing helps with both.

Which is better with children, Labrador or Golden?

Both are typically excellent with children when socialised. Goldens are often described as slightly gentler; Labradors can be more boisterous when young. Supervision is important with any dog.

Which is more expensive in the UK, Labrador or Golden Retriever?

Golden Retriever puppies often command slightly higher prices (£1,200–£2,800) than Labradors (£1,000–£2,500). Ongoing costs are similar.

Do Labradors and Golden Retrievers have similar exercise needs?

Yes. Both need at least 1–2 hours of exercise per day and thrive with active families.
