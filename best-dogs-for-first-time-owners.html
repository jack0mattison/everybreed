Best Dogs for First-Time Owners (2026)

First-time dog owners in the UK should look for breeds that are forgiving of mistakes, relatively easy to train and not overly demanding in terms of exercise or grooming. The best first dogs are typically friendly, biddable and adaptable.

The breeds below are often recommended for new owners. Remember: any dog needs time, patience and commitment. Puppy classes and consistent positive reinforcement will set you both up for success.

1. Labrador Retriever

Eager to please, food-motivated and generally easy to train. Needs lots of exercise and sheds heavily.

Size: Medium–Large · Exercise: High · Grooming: Moderate

2. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

Gentle, affectionate and not overly demanding. A good choice for a first small dog.

Size: Small · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: Moderate

3. Cockapoo

Friendly, sociable and usually responsive to training. Low shedding is a bonus for many first-time owners.

Size: Small–Medium · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: Moderate–High

4. Golden Retriever

Patient and trainable. Suits owners who can provide plenty of exercise and don't mind grooming.

Size: Large · Exercise: High · Grooming: Moderate

5. Whippet

Calm indoors, easy to groom and generally undemanding. Needs off-lead runs but not constant activity.

Size: Medium · Exercise: Moderate–High · Grooming: Low

6. Pug

Companionable and low exercise needs. Be aware of brachycephalic health issues and heat sensitivity.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: Low

7. Miniature Schnauzer

Lively but trainable. Requires regular grooming but is a loyal, adaptable small dog.

Size: Small · Exercise: Moderate · Grooming: High

8. Shih Tzu

Affectionate lap dog. Needs daily coat care but is generally easy-going and suited to quieter homes.

Size: Small · Exercise: Low–Moderate · Grooming: High

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest dog for a first-time owner?

Labradors, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels and Cockapoos are often cited as among the easiest for first-time owners because they are friendly, trainable and adaptable. Choose a breed that fits your lifestyle and space.
