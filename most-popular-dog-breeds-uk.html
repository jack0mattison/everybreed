The Most Popular Dog Breeds in the UK (2026)

EveryBreed · 2026

Which dog breeds are the most popular in the UK? Kennel Club registration figures and search trends give a good picture of the nation's favourites. Here we list the top 20 breeds and link to our full guides so you can learn more about temperament, size, health and UK costs.

Top 20 most popular dog breeds in the UK (2026)

  1. Labrador Retriever — The UK's number one for decades. Friendly, active and versatile.
  2. Cockapoo — Hugely popular crossbreed; friendly and low-shedding.
  3. French Bulldog — Compact, characterful and in high demand (and high price).
  4. Cocker Spaniel — Cheerful gundog and family favourite.
  5. Golden Retriever — Gentle, loyal and perennially popular.
  6. German Shepherd — Intelligent and loyal; needs experienced owners.
  7. Miniature Dachshund — Small, sturdy and full of character.
  8. English Springer Spaniel — Energetic gundog and family dog.
  9. Staffordshire Bull Terrier — Often called the "nanny dog"; loyal and muscular.
  10. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel — Gentle small companion.
  11. Border Collie — Brilliant but demanding; needs lots of activity.
  12. Miniature Schnauzer — Lively small dog with a distinctive look.
  13. Pug — Companionable and low exercise; be aware of breathing and heat.
  14. Whippet — Calm indoors, fast outdoors; easy to groom.
  15. Hungarian Vizsla — Athletic gundog for active owners.
  16. Border Terrier — Hardy, friendly small terrier.
  17. Shih Tzu — Affectionate lap dog; needs coat care.
  18. Boxer — Playful and energetic; good with family.
  19. Rottweiler — Confident and strong; needs early socialisation.
  20. Dalmatian — Striking looks and high energy.

Trends and crossbreeds

Designer crossbreeds such as Labradoodle, Goldendoodle and Cavapoo don't appear in Kennel Club registration stats (they're not recognised breeds) but are extremely popular in the UK and often feature in search and social trends. They typically command high puppy prices and need regular grooming.
